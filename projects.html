In spring 2014, I researched and implemented a supervised learning system for the iCub robot simulator with my classmate Kate Derosier, now a neuroscience PhD student at UCSF. Inspired by research from the Centre for Cognitive Science at Comenius University, we modeled a biologically inspired mirror neuron system for imitative learning. You can read more about our work here.
For my first hackathon, NYUHacks 2013, I designed an app for Facebook that randomly replaces the names of characters from a famous work of literature with the names of the user's top friends. Face Libs'' has a Javascript frontend and a natural language processing engine written in Python using NLTK, the natural language toolkit. HTML and design work was done by my collaborator Rachel Stein. The source is available on Github.
I am a committer on Charcoal, a C dialect for cooperative threading currently in production/research stage. Learn more about it here or on Github.
In the summer of 2013, I worked at the CMU Robotics Institute in planetary robotics. I investigated planning paths for a lunar rover based on solar energy and temperature states. You can read about the technical details in my poster.
In spring 2013, I participated in a semester-long seminar on humanoid robotics at Swarthmore College. Our assignments built up to a physically plausible ZMP walking simulator for a humanoid robot. My collaborator David Nahmias and I documented our work here and here.
